# Measures-of-the-Spatial-Structure-of-Urban-Agglomerations
This study aims to calculate the spatial structure index of major urban agglomerations in China from 2006 to 2022 based on the law of scale of order.
This project aims to conduct quantitative measurements and predictive analyses of the spatial structures of major urban agglomerations in China.By integrating population and 
economic (GDP) data and calculating the q-value using the Rank-Size Rule, the study evaluates the compactness, equilibrium, and spatial structural evolution characteristics of 
these urban agglomerations.
Based on multi-source data, this study measures the spatial structure of more than 20 national-level urban agglomerations, including the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei, Yangtze River Delta, and Pearl River Delta regions. The core workflow includes:
Data integration: Processing Excel data comprising multiple sheets, covering city panel data from different years.
Structural measurement: Calculating rank-size indices for the population dimension (qM) and the economic dimension (qN).
Comprehensive evaluation: Constructing a composite spatial structure index to quantify the spatial organizational efficiency of urban agglomerations.
